A very short introduction to natural deduction

We have defined how a well formed formula looks like in propositional logic. But we still can‘t prove anything, we still can‘t infer anything from a formula. Natural deduction is a collection of proof rules, which can be used to proof the validity of sequents.

Basic rules

We write down some examples of basic rules as valid sequents, which we don‘t have to prove anymore. We introduce and define top and bottom on this occasion.

  • a ∧ b ⊢ a (∧ elimination1)
  • a ∧ b ⊢ b (∧ elimination2)
  • a, b ⊢ a ∧ b (∧ introduction)
  • a ⊢ a ∨ b (∨ introduction1)
  • b ⊢ a ∨ b (∨ introduction2)
  • p ∧ ¬p ⊢ ⊥ (bottom)
  • p ∨ ¬p ⊢ ⊤ (top)

Examples

  • a ∧ b ⊢ a, b
  • a ∧ b ⊢ b
  • a, b ⊢ a ∧ b
  • p ∧ ¬p ⊢ ⊥ (bottom)
  • p ∨ ¬p ⊢ ⊤ (top)

Equality

Two formulas P and Q are equal if P ⊢ Q and Q ⊢ P are valid sequents.

Examples

  • a ∧ b = b ∧ a
  • a ∧ a = a
  • a ∨ a = a
