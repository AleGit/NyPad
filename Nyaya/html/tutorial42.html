Negation Normal Form

a b ¬(a ∧ b) ≡ ¬a ∨ ¬b
T T F = F
T F T = T
F T T = T
F F T = T

A propositional formula in negation normal form does not contain implications, negations of conjunctions or negations of disjunctions. A negation normal form contains negations of atoms, conjunctions and disjunctions.

P  ::=  p  |  ¬p  |  (P ∧ P)  |  (P ∨ P)
a b ¬(a ∨ b) ≡ ¬a ∧ ¬b
T T F = F
T F F = F
F T F = F
F F T = T

Still we do not want to reduce the expressiveness of our formulas. We substitute negations of conjunctions and negations of disjunctions with equivalent formulas. To do that we use De Morgan‘s laws.

¬(a ∧ b) ≡ ¬a ∨ ¬b
¬(a ∨ b) ≡ ¬a ∧ ¬b
a ≡ ¬¬a
T = T
F = F

While substituting implications and negations of conjunctions or disjunctions we easily get double negations of atoms, which are not allowed in negation normal forms. Double negations can be substituted with equivalent expressions.

¬¬a ≡ a

Examples

  • a ∧ b → a ≡ ¬(a ∧ b) ∨ a ≡ (¬a ∨ ¬b) ∨ b = ¬a ∨ ¬b ∨ a
  • a → b → a ≡ a → (¬b ∨ a) ≡ ¬a ∨ (¬b ∨ a) ≡ ¬a ∨ b ∨ a
  • (a → b) → a ≡ (¬a ∨ b) → a ≡ ¬(¬a ∨ b) ∨ a ≡ (¬¬a ∧ b) ∨ a ≡ (a ∧ b) ∨ a
  • ¬a → b ≡ ¬¬a ∨ b ≡ a ∨ b
  • ¬(a → b) ≡ ¬(¬a ∨ b) ≡ ¬¬a ∧ ¬b ≡ a ∧ ¬b

Note that (¬a ∨ ¬b) ∨ b and ¬a ∨ ¬b ∨ a in the first example are equal formulas. We remove the parentheses because of syntax conventions. Note that ¬a ∨ (¬b ∨ a) and ¬a ∨ b ∨ a in the second example are equivalent formulas. We remove the parentheses because we get a semantically equivalent formula.

We observe that negation normal forms look easier to evaluate.
