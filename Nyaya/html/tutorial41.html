Chapter 4
Normal Forms

Implication Free Form

For simpler argumentation over properties of propositional formulas, we define a equivalent subset of forms – the normal forms. We start with the implication free form, which is a propositional formula which does not include any implications. The grammar for implication free forms is

P  ::=  p  |  (¬P)  |  (P ∧ P)  |  (P ∨ P)
a b a → b ≡ ¬a ∨ b
T T T = T
T F F = F
F T T = T
F F T = T

We do not want to reduce the expressiveness of our formulas so we substitute implications with equivalent expressions.

P → Q ≡ ¬P ∨ Q

Examples

  • a ∧ b → a ≡ ¬(a ∧ b) ∨ a
  • a → b → a ≡ a → (¬b ∨ a) ≡ ¬a ∨ (¬b ∨ a)
  • (a → b) → a ≡ (¬a ∨ b) → a ≡ ¬(¬a ∨ b) ∨ a
  • ¬a → b ≡ ¬¬a ∨ b
  • ¬(a → b) ≡ ¬(¬a ∨ b)

We observe that we introduce additional negations to our formulas.
