Disjunctive Normal Form

A literal is an atom or the negation of an atom. A propositional formula in disjunctive normal form (DNF) is a disjunction of clauses, where each clause is a conjunction of literals.

L  ::=  p  |  ¬p
C  ::=  L  |  L ∨ C
P  ::=  C  |  C ∧ P
DNF
a ∧ (b ∨ c) ≡ (a ∧ b) ∨ (a ∧ c)
(a ∨ b) ∧ c ≡ (a ∧ c) ∨ (b ∧ c)

By definition a DNF is implication free and in negation normal form.

To transform a arbitrary formula into CNF, we transform the formula into NNF, then we distribute conjunctions over disjunctions.

Examples

Satisfiability

A conjunctive clause of literals is satisfiable (evaluates to true for at least one truth assignment) if it does not contain a pair of complementary literals. A disjunction of clauses is satisfiable if at least one clause is satisfiable.

A DNF is satisfiable it it least one clause does not contain a pair of complementary literals.
