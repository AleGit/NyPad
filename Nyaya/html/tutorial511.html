Boolean Expressions

Precedences

The complement has the highest precedence. «ā•b», «ā+b» and «ā⊕b» are to be interpreted as «(ā)•b», «(ā)+b» and «(ā)⊕b». The product has a higher precedence than the sum and the exclusive or – «a•b+c» means «(a•b)+c» and «a•b⊕c means «(a•b)⊕c». The sum has a higher precedence than the exclusive or – «a+b⊕c» is to be read as «(a+b)⊕c».

Does a•(b+c) = a•b+a•c look right, but a+b•c = (a+b)•(a+c) does not? Indeed, the second equivalence does not hold for arithmetic expressions, but both Boolean expression represent the same Boolean function.

Equivalence of Boolean expressions

Two Boolean expressions are equivalent if they represent the same Boolean expression.
