Syntax

Conventions

The use of parentheses has some advantages

  • The grammar is simple. It consists of four recursive rules and one rule for atoms.
  • Two formulas are syntactically equal when their string representations are exactly the same.

But there is a drawback. We have to write many parentheses. This reduces the readability of formulas for humans. To save some parenthesis we will omit outer parentheses and we will use associativity and precedence to omit inner parentheses.

Negations and implications are right associative, while conjunctions and disjunctions are left associative. Negations have the highest precedence. Conjunctions have a higher precedence than disjunctions (this is not a general convention). Implications have the lowest precedence.

With these conventions different strings can represent the syntactically same propositional formula.

Do not hesitate to use parenthesis to emphasize the structure of a formula, especially when conjunctions are used in combination with disjunctions.

a ∨ b ∧ c ∧ d ∧ e ∧ f ∨ g ∧ h
vs.
a ∨ (b ∧ c ∧ d ∧ e ∧ f) ∨ (g ∧ h)
