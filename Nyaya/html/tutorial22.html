Conventions

The use of parenthesis has some advantages

  • The grammar is simple. It consits of four recursive rules and one rule for atoms.
  • Two formulas are equal when their string represenations are exactly the same.

But there is a drawback. We have two write many parenthesis, which reduces the readability of formulas for humans. To save some parenthesis we will ommit outer parenthesis and we will use associativity and precedence.

Negations and implications are right associative, while conjunctions and disjunctions are left associative. Negations have the highest precedence. Conjunctions has a higher precedence than disjunctions. Implications has the lowest precedence.

With these conventions different strings can represent syntactically the same propositional formula.

Do not hesitate to use parenthesis to emphisize the structure of a formula, especially when conjunctions are used in combination with disjunctions.

a ∨ b ∧ c ∧ d ∧ e ∧ f ∨ g ∧ h
vs.
a ∨ (b ∧ c ∧ d ∧ e ∧ f) ∨ (g ∧ h)
