Hidden compostions

To “translate“ natural sentences into atoms or compositions is not always unambiguous. You can treat “Elephants are big, grey animals“ as one simple sentence, and represent it with “p” (an atom). Or you can decompose it to “Elephants are big and elephants are grey and elephants are animals” which can be modelled as “p and q and r” (an composite). This decomposition of sentences in natural language is part of the modelling process and can be very hard.
