Limits of Propositional Logic

Propositional logic hat limits. But it is not easy to argue, why propositional logic can not be used for a specific situation, when our guts tell us the opposite.

All men are mortal

Check the composite sentence:

All men are mortal. Sokrates was a man. Therefore, Sokrates was mortal.

This feels right, but from the structure we have no argument, because the structure

p. q. Therefore r.

does not hold in all situations:

The sky is blue. The sea is green. Therefore, I am late.

Of course we can try to rewrite, but which argument do we have to do that? And can it be done in all situations? Did we do it correctly?

If man than mortal. If sokrates than man. Sokrates. Therefore, mortal.
If sky than blue. If sea than green. Me. Therefore, late.

Which leads to two different structures.

If p than q. If s than p. s. Therefore q. • If p than q. If s than r. s. Therefore t.
