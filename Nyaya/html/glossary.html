Precedence

NOT⊐ AND⊐ OR⊐ XOR⊐ BIC⊐ IMP
¬⊐ ∧⊐ ∨⊐ ⊻⊐ ↔⊐ →
!⊐ .⊐ +⊐ ⊕
⊐ &⊐ |⊐ ^ <>⊐ >

Precedence of connectives is used to get rid of too many parenthesis. The artihmetic expression a.b+c is to be read as (a.b)+c, while a.(b+c) can’t be written without parenthesis. This is because by convention mutltiplication has a higher precedence than addition. Similar conventions are used in propositional logic.

Precedence determines the syntax tree, i.e. the order of valuation of a composite formula. A symbol with higher precedence is evaluated before a symbol with lower precedence. Atoms in propositional formual has the highest precedence implicitly because the must be evaluated before connectives can be evaluated.
