Associativity

A binary connective is associative when the evaluation order does not matter for the result.

Precedence

Precedence of connectives is used to get rid of too many parentheses. The artihmetic expression a.b+c is to be read as (a.b)+c, while a.(b+c) can’t be written without parentheses. This is because by convention mutltiplication has a higher precedence than addition. Similar conventions are used in propositional logic.

Precedence determines the order of valuation of a composite formula. A symbol with higher precedence is evaluated before a symbol with lower precedence. Implicitly atoms in propositional formulas has the highest precedence because they must be evaluated before connectives can be evaluated. Most connectives are associative, but not the implication (→).
