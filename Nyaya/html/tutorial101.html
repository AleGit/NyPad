Applications of Propositional Logic

Everyday life

The aim of logic in general is to reason about situations. [LICS,1]:

If the train arrives late and there are no taxis at the station, then John is late for his meeting. John is not late for his meeting. The train did arrive late. Therefore, there were taxis a the station.
If it is raining and Jane does not have her umbrella with her, then she will get wet. Jane is not wet. It is raining. Therefore, Jan has her ubrella with her.

We build 18 basic sentences (propositions) to describe a tic-tac-toe table. For “cell A holds O” we write “ao”, for “cell A holds X” we write “ax”, … ao ax bo bx co cx do dx eo ex fo fx go gx jo jx. The partially filled table can be discribed as “ao and not ax and not bo and not bx and not co and cx and not do and not dx and eo and not ex and not fo and not fx and not go and gx and not ho and hx and jo and not jx”. There are some conditions that must be fullfilled. If A holds O, then A does not hold X. If ao then not ax. If ax then not ao. … which are fullfilled.

Now we can check for a winner: If (ao and bo and co) or (ao and eo and jo) or (ao and do and go) or (bo and eo and ho) or (co and fo and jo) or (co and eo and go) or (do and eo and fo) or (go and ho and jo) then oiw. We derive ao, eo, jo from the filling. Therefore, oiw.
